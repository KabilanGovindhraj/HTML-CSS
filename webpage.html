Historical Places in India

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the Yamuna river in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan, to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

Red Fort

The Red Fort is a historic fort in the city of Delhi. It served as the main residence of the Mughal Emperors. The fort showcases a fusion of Persian, Indian, and European architectural styles.

Hampi

Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the state of Karnataka. It was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire and is known for its ancient temples, palaces, and stone-carved monuments.
